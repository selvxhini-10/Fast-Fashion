A Timeline of Fast Fashion

November 19, 2022 by Isabel Liu

1800s

  • Cycle of fashion picked up due to the industrial revolution as it introduced new textile machines, factories, ready-made clothing/bulk clothing.
  • Sewing machine first patented in 1846, and the invention contributed to a rapid fall in clothing price and increase in manufacturing scale.
  • Some aspects of production for dressmaking businesses outsourced parts of production.
  • To “sweaters”, people who worked from home for low wages. This was a glimpse of basis of modern clothing production.

1900s-1950s

  • WW2 led to increase in standardised clothing production
  • Because of fabric restrictions and need for more functional styles
  • After being accustomed to this, consumers became more receptive to buying standardised clothing after the war too

1960s-2000s

  • In the 1960s, young people embraced cheaply made clothing to follow fashion trends.
  • Fashion brands had to keep up with this increased demand for affordable clothing.
  • Led to textile mills opening around the world (outsourced labour).
  • Fashion brands had to prioritise affordability and speed.
  • The term “fast fashion’ was first used to summarise Zara’s mission. Making speed the driving force, declaring it would only take 15 days for a garment to go from being in a designer’s brain to being sold on the racksPrevious stores that used to be popular slowly fell out because they couldn’t produce “trendy” clothing fast enough in comparison to other brands
  • Previous stores that used to be popular slowly fell out because they couldn’t produce “trendy” clothing fast enough in comparison to other brands
  • Fast fashion brands were also seen endorsed. Leading ladies such as Kate Middleton and Michelle Obama wearing dresses from Zara and H&M

Present day

  • Fast fashion is very intertwined with capitalism — outsourcing cheap labour to create clothing to sell by major fast fashion brands
  • Trends are changing extremely quickly because of the ability of fast fashion
  • No longer trendy clothing often gets replaced and thrown out by new trendy pieces, and this creates a lot of waste and environmental damage.

While the fashion industry has an extensive history, fast fashion is a relatively new concept that began with the mass production of cheap clothing. It recently became popular due to its cheap and efficient manufacturing processes, causing an increase in consumer demands.

About

A brief history about fast fashion will provide us with the tools to identify and analyze the problem further. It gives us crucial insights and perspectives about the past to enhance our current understanding.
