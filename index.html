Our Story

What is Fast Fashion?
Fast Fashion can be described in three words: cheap, trendy and desirable. Some of our loveable fashion brands including SHEIN, Forever 21, H&M, Fashion Nova and Zara are known for their affordable prices and stylish clothing options for consumers. These retailers continue to significantly impact the fashion industry as there’s an increasing demand for new and trendy clothing.



But why does this matter?
Fast fashion brands are namely responsible for the many detrimental social and environmental impacts of overproduction and overconsumption of cheap, disposable clothing. As clothing is inspired by fashion runway shows and social media influencers and sold at affordable prices, consumers are encouraged to purchase and dispose of more due to the changing trends.

The result: fast fashion is one of the largest polluters in the world.
It’s responsible for textile waste, clean water pollution, toxic chemicals, carbon emissions, child labour, slavery and more. But how can we help? As consumers, we hold tremendous power to stop this from happening, as the success of fast fashion can be attributed to the increasing demand of consumers. We chose this topic in the hopes of shedding more light on this issue and informing our audience about the importance of supporting our cause.
