Ways to Help

This page will strive to inform you (the reader) of ways to help mitigate the impacts of fast fashion. The tips outlined in this article serve as a model for what we hope to be a potential solution to this problem.

Our Solution

December 11, 2022 by Noor Alharam

Buy more from sustainable brands. Use sustainable fabrics like silk, organic cotton, linen, hemp and lyocell. When shopping, consider quality over quantity. Be mindful of whether the manufacturer uses sustainable and ethical labour practices. Purchase second-hand clothing to be eco-friendlier. Shop local. Wash your clothes in a correct manner...

It's evident that we can work towards solving this issue by taking the time to rectify the small mistakes we make in our daily lives. And these are tips you most likely hear all the time.

But we don't wish to tell you generic solutions that have little to no impact. Instead, we propose an alternative solution — one that explores the use of technology and how it can be utilized to minimize our waste and reduce our carbon footprint.

Radio-frequency identification (RFID)

RFID tags are a sort of tracking device that employs radio frequency to search for, identify, track, and connect with goods and people. They enable users to automatically and securely identify and track inventories and assets. RFID advances auto-ID technology by allowing tags to be read without line of sight and, depending on the kind of RFID, having a read range ranging from a few centimetres to over 20 metres.

RFID in fast fashion allows merchants to track goods more accurately throughout the supply chain, allowing them to make more informed decisions and spot issues. When fashion inventory is tagged at the moment of manufacture, its journey may be followed to a high degree of precision throughout the supply chain.

RFID tags are classified into two types: passive and active. The most prevalent are passive RFID tags, which don’t require a direct line of sight to a reader but have a significantly limited read range, are smaller in size, and are lightweight. Passive RFID tags are excellent for the following applications:

  • Asset and employee tracking
  • Clothing and retail
  • Inventory and supply chain management
  • Logistics
  • Anti-counterfeiting and brand protection
  • Pharmaceutical and medical care
  • Control of gate and perimeter access
  • Travel and entertainment

Moreover, it is also feasible to scan huge volumes of stock at once, simplifying the procedure and providing instantaneous data. RFID can also potentially make supply chains more robust by disclosing operational flaws. As a result, supply chain choices may be made more rapidly and with more precise data to back them up.

RFID also allows shops to embrace sustainable solutions such as fashion rental. When RFID tags are installed on every fashion inventory, it is simple to execute returns and even categorize returned clothing for cleaning.

RFID in fast fashion can also assist in improving a retailer’s overall sustainability by boosting stock accuracy. Knowing which clothing items are selling and which aren’t allows businesses to respond swiftly to customer demand and order just what will sell.

Inventory loss is a major issue in the fashion industry, but can be handled with reliable data. Because RFID technology is so accurate, fast fashion businesses can only order what they need and be confident that they will not receive out-of-stock messages. So, RFID in fast fashion is an amazing retail option for firms searching for a low-cost high-return strategy to strengthen their ethics and improve their reputation.

Additional Information

  • The usage of radio frequency identification (RFID) in warehouses or retail enterprises can help acquire a more accurate and better understanding of a company’s stock levels. This can potentially save the company from wasting so many products and clothes.
  • Warehouses are also more likely to transport proper items to the correct place and in the exact quantity. So coordinating the fashion products they offer becomes much easier when they deliver items to the proper places and know what’s selling in which regions. So RFID is a fantastic way to use advancements in technology to coordinate product movement.
  • This not only helps things to move faster between the warehouse and retailers, but it also significantly reduces waste caused by sending products to the wrong location or sending too many products for a merchant to sell profitably.
  • In addition to RFID, the company can also employ Artificial Intelligence to estimate product quantities. This is more commonly utilized by retailers than by warehouses or fast fashion designers, although it can be used at all stages of production and sales. The company can avoid overstocking by using AI to offer information about the amount of merchandise it feels they will use or require.
  • The fast-fashion firm can also use AI to identify trends such as where to test new designs and deliver new clothes first, and to analyze sales before shipping to additional stores.
  • In-store AI alternatives also include viewing what is being tried on but not purchased in the fitting rooms. Those goods may appear attractive on the hanger, but they don’t fit well in reality.
  • If the business is selling quickly out of other clothing items or just specific sizes of particular clothing, they can use AI to help them decide what to order more of, and what to avoid purchasing in the future.
  • A sizing issue with trendy outfits might also result in a lot of waste since customers will not buy it even though it looks good on the hanger. But with enhanced AI suggestions and systems, including online style guides, the firm can eliminate online returns and the problem of customers ordering the same thing in several sizes, lowering shipping and allowing them to sell items while they are still in style.


Closing Remarks

Perhaps, this solution has the ability to beyond this Hackathon. But we are aware that while this doesn't address the entirety of the issue, it demonstrates that technology (and coding) can be used as a tool to solve global problems.
